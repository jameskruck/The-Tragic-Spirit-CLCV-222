Course Schedule

Below is the tentative schedule for the course. Readings and assignments are subject to change based on class discussions and needs.

Weeks 4-6: Justice and Transformation

  • Cycles of violence and justice
  • Maternal power and gender in tragedy
  • Readings: Aeschylus, The Furies; Butler on gender performance

Weeks 7-9: Fate and Individual Choice

  • Oedipus and the struggle with destiny
  • Antigone and civil disobedience
  • Readings: Sophocles, Oedipus the King; Judith Butler on Antigone

Weeks 10-13: Gender and Power

  • Medea and cultural identity
  • Bacchae and religious identity
  • Readings: Euripides, Medea; Bacchae; Hall on Medea’s identity

Weeks 14-16: Roman Reception and Contemporary Perspectives

  • Seneca's adaptation of Greek tragedy
  • Modern adaptations and performances
  • Readings: Seneca’s tragedies; Foley on modern performances

Final projects due in Week 16. Review sessions and student presentations will conclude the course.
