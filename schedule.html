Weeks 1-3: Introducing Tragedy and Cultural Perspectives
  • Week 1: Understanding tragic form and context
  • Critical approaches to ancient performance
  • Multiple cultural perspectives on tragedy
  • Readings: Selections from Nicole Loraux, "Tragic Ways of Killing a Woman"; Dan-el Padilla Peralta on marginalized voices.
  • Week 2: Justice and Gender in the Ancient World
  • Tragic perspectives on justice
  • Women's voices in ancient drama
  • Readings: Froma Zeitlin, "Playing the Other"; Edith Hall on tragic performance and cultural identity.
  • Week 3: Oresteia - Beginning the Cycle
  • Libation Bearers and vengeance
  • Family obligations across cultures
  • Maternal power and resistance
  • Readings: Aeschylus, Agamemnon (complete); bell hooks on tragedy and intersectionality.
Weeks 4-6: Justice and Transformation
  • Week 4: Oresteia - Cycles of Violence
  • Libation Bearers and vengeance
  • Family obligations across cultures
  • Maternal power and resistance
  • Readings: Aeschylus, Libation Bearers (complete); Patricia Johnson, "Blood-Stained Hands: Female Power and Ritual"
  • First Critical Analysis Project Due
  • Week 5: Oresteia - Justice Transformed
  • The Furies and social change
  • Transformation of justice systems
  • Gender and divine power
  • Readings: Aeschylus, The Furies (complete); Judith Butler on gender performance in tragedy
  • Global adaptations of the justice theme
  • Rebecca Bushnell on tragic justice across cultures
  • Week 6: The Oresteia in Modern Context
  • Contemporary productions
  • Cultural adaptations
  • Political interpretations
  • Readings: Selected modern adaptations; Helene Foley on tragic patterns in modern drama
  • First Reaction Paper Due
